It's easy to fall into a routine of heavy spices and complex marinades—a slow-cooked Thai red coconut curry here, some deeply seasoned Indian chicken there—but sometimes you just want something incredibly simple. If you want to skip the breading and the hot oil, Kurczak w Sosie Koperkowym (Polish Chicken in Dill Sauce) is exactly what you need.

Dill is a massive staple in Polish cuisine, and this dish is incredibly comforting. Instead of frying, the chicken is gently simmered in a rich, creamy, herb-packed broth. It’s light, fast, and takes about 20 minutes.

### Ingredients

* **1 lb** boneless, skinless chicken breasts (cut into bite-sized cubes)
* **1 tablespoon** butter or olive oil (just to soften the veggies)
* **1 small** onion, finely chopped
* **1 medium** carrot, grated or finely diced
* **1 cup** chicken broth
* **1/2 cup** heavy cream (or full-fat sour cream)
* **1 tablespoon** all-purpose flour
* **1 large bunch** of fresh dill, finely chopped (about 1/2 cup—don't skimp here!)
* **1/2 teaspoon** salt
* **1/4 teaspoon** black pepper

### Instructions

1.  **Sauté the aromatics:** Heat the butter or oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and grated carrot. Cook for about 3 to 4 minutes until the onion is soft and translucent.
2.  **Brown the chicken:** Add the cubed chicken to the pan. Season with salt and pepper. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, just until the outside of the chicken is no longer pink (it doesn't need a hard sear or a crust).
3.  **Simmer:** Pour in the chicken broth. Bring the liquid to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 10 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
4.  **Thicken the sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and the flour until smooth. *Tip: If using sour cream, stir a few spoonfuls of the hot chicken broth into the sour cream first so it doesn't curdle when it hits the hot pan.*
5.  **Finish the dish:** Pour the cream mixture into the skillet, stirring constantly. Let it simmer uncovered for another 2 to 3 minutes until the sauce thickens nicely.
6.  **Add the dill:** Remove the pan from the heat and stir in all the fresh chopped dill. Taste the sauce and add a little more salt and pepper if needed.

### How to serve it

This is incredibly saucy, so you'll want something to soak it up. It is traditionally served over plain white rice, boiled baby potatoes, or Polish dumplings like *kopytka* (similar to gnocchi).
