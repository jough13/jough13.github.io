How the Hybrid Engine Works

Traditional Elo systems only look at wins and losses, ignoring the chaotic nature of soccer where the better team frequently loses to a lucky bounce. Pure Expected Goals (xG) models ignore the fact that actually finishing chances matters.

This Hybrid xG-Elo system solves this by blending the two. Every MLS match dating back to 2013 is recalculated to establish a highly accurate historical baseline.

The Formulas

1. Expected Performance (E):
E = 1 / (1 + 10^((Rating_Away - (Rating_Home + 25)) / 400))

2. Blended Actual Performance (A):
A = (0.70 * Match_Result) + (0.30 * xG_Share)
*Match Result = 1 (Win), 0.5 (Draw), 0 (Loss)

3. Rating Update:
New_Rating = Old_Rating + [32 * Blowout_Multiplier * (A - E)]

Example Calculation

The Scenario: Two evenly matched teams (both rated 1500) play. The Home Team wins 2-0 and dominates the chances, posting 2.0 xG to the Away Team's 0.5 xG.

Step 1: Expectations
Because of the +25 Home Field Advantage boost, the model expects the Home Team to control the game slightly, giving them an Expected Performance (E) of 0.536.

Step 2: Actual Performance
The Home Team won the game, earning a Match Result of 1.0. They also generated 80% of the total xG (2.0 out of 2.5).
Blended Performance (A) = (0.70 * 1.0) + (0.30 * 0.80) = 0.94

Step 3: The Rating Change
Because the Goal Differential was less than 3, the Blowout Multiplier is 1.0.
Point Change = 32 * 1.0 * (0.94 - 0.536) = +12.9 points.

Result: The Home Team rises to 1512.9, and the Away Team drops to 1487.1.
